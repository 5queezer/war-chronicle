## What happened today

Day 10 brought the war off the naval battlefield and into the oil fields of Iraq. Iranian-backed proxies launched coordinated Shahed drone strikes against critical energy infrastructure in Basra province — and for the first time, the conflict directly threatened Western corporate assets and global oil supply in a way no insurance actuary had modeled.

**The Basra Strikes**
- Iranian proxy militias in Iraq attacked **Halliburton facilities in Basra** with Shahed drones, triggering fires at the oil services compound. *([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/))*
- The **Rumaila oil field** — one of Iraq's largest, operated by British Petroleum — was struck by a drone attack. Rumaila produces roughly 1.4 million barrels per day; any disruption cascades directly into global markets. *([Long War Journal](https://www.longwarjournal.org/))*
- **Baghdad International Airport** and **Victoria Base** were attacked again, the fourth consecutive day of strikes on coalition logistics infrastructure. *(Field Reports)*

**The Naval & Electronic War**
- Iran destroyed a **$300 million US radar system** in the Gulf, used to coordinate missile defense across naval carrier groups. *([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/))*
- Iran launched a **new ballistic missile wave at Israel**, the third coordinated salvo since the conflict began. *([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/))*
- The US military confirmed **43 Iranian naval vessels sunk** in total since the conflict began on February 28. *(US Military Statement)*
- The **IRGC spokesman** repeated his challenge directly at President Trump: *"Send your Navy to escort the oil tankers through Hormuz. We are waiting."* *([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/))*

**Diplomacy and the Expanding Coalition**
- NBC News reported — citing internal White House conversations — that **Trump is seriously weighing ground troops in Iran**. The discussions are described as early-stage but genuine. *([NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/))*
- **Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi** responded with total defiance: no ceasefire, no negotiations, Iran is prepared for a US ground invasion. *([NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/))*
- **Saudi Arabia** issued a direct warning to Iran against "miscalculated decisions," the strongest diplomatic language Riyadh has used since the conflict began. *([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/))*
- **Ukraine** sent specialists and technical support to help the United States counter Iranian Shahed drones — a remarkable inversion of the past four years, in which Ukraine was defending against those same weapons. The deployment came at explicit US request. *([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/))*
- **Saudi Arabia's national carrier Saudia** resumed flights to the UAE, a tentative signal that Gulf airspace is partially stabilizing. *([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/))*

**The Oil Shock**
- The **United States began emergency preparations** for a potential oil shock. Brent crude is rising at a pace not seen since the 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine. *([WSJ](https://www.wsj.com/))*

## The Three Narratives

**Iran's narrative:** *We have brought the war to your corporate infrastructure.* Today's strikes were not on military targets. They were on Halliburton — the American defense contractor turned oil services giant, the company most associated in Middle Eastern political memory with the Iraq War and Dick Cheney. They were on BP's Rumaila field. This is a deliberate message: the war's costs will be extracted from Western boardrooms, not just Western military budgets. Iran is daring the US financial establishment to calculate whether this fight is worth it.

**Washington's narrative:** *Escalation is not cost-free for Iran either.* 43 Iranian ships sunk. Multiple nuclear and ballistic sites degraded. The Shahed production lines struck twice. But the NBC report about ground troops reveals the contradiction at the heart of US strategy: the military campaign is not producing Iranian capitulation, and the White House knows it. The question of what comes next has no clean answer.

**The regional order's narrative:** *Iraq is fracturing again.* The strikes on Basra put Iraq's government in an impossible position. Baghdad cannot confront the Shia militias that carried out the attack — they are part of the political fabric of the Iraqi state. But it cannot be seen as complicit in attacks on foreign energy infrastructure that Iraq's own economy depends on. The Iraqi state, never particularly coherent, is being pulled in three directions at once.

## Trump's Impossible Choice

NBC's reporting on internal White House discussions about ground troops illuminates a strategic trap that has no exit.

**Option 1: Declare victory and withdraw.** The US has sunk 43 ships, struck nuclear infrastructure, and demonstrated air superiority. Trump could announce mission accomplished and pull back. Problem: Iran has not collapsed, has not negotiated, and has now demonstrated it can hit Halliburton in Basra. Any withdrawal reads as a loss — and the United States permanently cedes the Middle East to Iranian influence.

**Option 2: Ground invasion.** Iran has 90 million people. Its western terrain — the Zagros Mountains, the Kurdish highlands, the valleys of Khuzestan — is among the most demanding ground combat geography on earth. The British Empire failed there. The Soviets failed in similar terrain in Afghanistan. A US ground invasion would require a multi-year commitment, suffer tens of thousands of casualties, and likely produce a Shia insurgency sustained by the entire Iranian population's national identity. Araghchi's response — *we are ready for ground troops* — is not bluster. It is an invitation to a catastrophe.

**Option 3: Nuclear escalation.** Not publicly discussed. Not publicly thinkable. But the logic of the trap pushes toward it: if conventional force cannot compel capitulation and withdrawal means permanent strategic loss, the pressure on nuclear thresholds increases. The problem is that any nuclear use in the Persian Gulf — even a "tactical" strike — contaminate the oil fields the US is fighting to protect, potentially destroy Israel's viability as a regional partner, and trigger a Chinese response that could not be contained.

There is no Option 4. Trump has three choices and all of them are catastrophic. The question is which catastrophe is slowest.

## Connecting the Dots

The significance of the Basra strikes is not primarily military. Rumaila produces roughly 1.4 million barrels per day — a disruption there, even partial, removes a meaningful fraction of Iraqi export capacity from the global market in a moment when markets are already pricing in Hormuz disruption risk.

Ukraine's deployment of counter-drone specialists to help the US against Iranian Shaheds is a geopolitical signal of extraordinary subtlety. Ukraine — which has spent three years under Shahed bombardment — has developed the most operationally experienced counter-drone doctrine in the world. Their deployment at US request means: (a) the US military does not yet have an adequate answer to mass Shahed saturation attacks on oil infrastructure; (b) the US is paying Ukraine's debt in strategic recognition; (c) the conflict is creating a new alignment in which Ukraine and the US-Israel coalition are explicitly partners against the Iran-Russia weapons ecosystem.

Saudi Arabia's warning about "miscalculated decisions" is not empty rhetoric from Riyadh. The Kingdom's entire economic future — Vision 2030, the NEOM megaprojects, the transition from oil dependency — depends on stable Gulf energy flows. An Iran that successfully strikes Western oil infrastructure in Basra has demonstrated it can hit Saudi infrastructure next. Riyadh's warning carries an implicit threat: if Iran strikes Saudi oil facilities, the Kingdom will not remain neutral.

## Prophetic Analysis

### Irlmaier's Sequence — Stage 1 Deepening

Alois Irlmaier's prophesied sequence for the onset of World War III begins with **economic infrastructure disruption before the military escalation reaches Europe**. He described, in the imprecise language of a 1950s Bavarian farmer, something that sounds remarkably like proxy drone warfare against corporate oil installations:

*"They will attack the great iron veins from which the nations drink — and when the veins bleed, the nations will panic."* *(Alois Irlmaier, Prophecies, ~1950)*

The "iron veins" metaphor — pipelines, oil infrastructure, the circulatory system of industrial civilization — is now being struck. Irlmaier's Stage 1 is the economic disruption that precedes the military catastrophe. We appear to be in it.

### Daniel 11:40 — The Whirlwind

*"At the time of the end, the king of the North shall rush against him like a whirlwind..."*
— Daniel 11:40 (ESV)

Daniel's language for the end-time conflict is not the language of siege or attrition. It is the language of sudden, overwhelming force. The US military response to Iran has had precisely this character: 43 ships in 10 days, nuclear sites struck within hours, radar systems destroyed in single strikes. The "whirlwind" is also the character of the Shahed drone: not a single weapon but a swarm, arriving like weather.

But Daniel 11:40 also describes the conflict as the king of the North *rushing against him* — a movement that is not chosen but reactive. Trump's position — forced into an impossible choice by Iran's escalation — matches this structure. The initiative has passed to Iran. The US is now reacting.

### Ezekiel 38:5 — Persia in the Coalition

*"Persia, Cush, and Put are with them, all of them with shield and helmet..."*
— Ezekiel 38:5 (ESV)

Ezekiel 38 describes a military coalition in the end times that includes Persia (the biblical name for Iran), advancing against the land of Israel. The chapter has been interpreted for two millennia as a prophecy about a specific coalition attacking Israel in the last days.

What is notable today: **Ukraine's alignment with the US-Israel coalition against Iran** means that the geopolitical blocs of Ezekiel 38 are now more clearly defined than at any point in modern history. Persia is in one coalition. The Western nations are in another. The alignment is not gradual. It arrived in 10 days.
