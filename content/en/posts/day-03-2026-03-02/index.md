## What happened today

- Iran attacked US military bases in Azerbaijan and Qatar — expanding the war beyond Israel's borders.
- Six US soldiers were killed in these strikes — the first confirmed American combat deaths of the conflict. *(Pentagon)*
- Airspace over the entire Gulf region was disrupted; commercial flights rerouted or cancelled.
- Protests broke out in Tehran — ordinary Iranians taking to the streets against the regime even as the war intensifies.
- Iran's partial blockade of the Strait of Hormuz has begun.

## Intelligence & Field Reports

- A senior IRGC General warned: *"Within a few days, US bases in the Middle East will have no way to defend against incoming Iranian missiles and drones." *(IRNA)*
- The **Rotana Hotel in Erbil**, Iraqi Kurdistan, was struck by an Iranian drone — confirming Iran's willingness to target civilian-adjacent infrastructure across multiple countries beyond the direct combat zone. *(Reuters)*
- Qatar announced **partial resumption of air traffic**, following the Gulf-wide airspace disruption triggered by Day 3 Iranian strikes — signaling the conflict's direct impact on regional commercial aviation. *(Reuters)*
- Iran struck additional targets in **Iraqi Kurdistan and the Persian Gulf** on the same day, broadening its operational theater beyond Azerbaijan and Qatar. *(IRNA)*

## Where things stand

Iran has chosen to go wide rather than deep — striking US infrastructure across two countries to stretch American response capacity. The first American deaths will increase domestic pressure on the White House to hit harder. Meanwhile, the regime faces its own population on the streets of Tehran.

## What to watch

- Will the US respond with a major escalation after soldier deaths?
- Will the Tehran protests grow into something that threatens the regime from inside?
